Featured Research

Endogenous Agglomeration via Topological Field Theory

Frontier Theory Persistent Homology PDE Solver Python

The Core Innovation: Derives Topological Pressure Πtopo from persistent homology as a variational force that generates endogenous city formation without explicit agglomeration externalities—solving Starrett's (1978) spatial impossibility theorem.

Key Results:

  • Topological Turing Instability: Homogeneous equilibrium destabilizes at λ > λc, selecting characteristic city scale ℓ = 2π/kmax
  • Topological Gravity Equation: Migration flows v = χ∇(y + λK*y) exhibit nonlocal "tunneling" between distant hubs absent from standard models
  • Empirical Framework: Three testable predictions using BACI trade data, gravity residuals, and network resilience analysis

Technical Implementation: Complete production codebase with FFT-based PDE solver, persistence diagram computation (gudhi/ripser), power spectrum analysis, and empirical testing pipeline. 860 LOC core dynamics.

Climate Energy Externalities & Winter Storm Uri

Spatial Econometrics Event Study GIS Co-authored

Lead Author: David Park (high school student, Crean Lutheran) | PI & Advisor: Dr. Ian Helfrich

Research Questions: Do renewable energy facilities create spatial externalities? What was the distributional impact of Winter Storm Uri (Feb 2021) on Texas communities?

Methods & Results:

  • Spatial Autocorrelation: Corrected k-NN Moran's I analysis reveals significant spatial clustering in renewable energy externalities
  • Winter Storm Uri Event Study: Causal identification of storm impact using difference-in-differences framework with panel fixed effects (T=5 years)
  • Energy Distributional Index (EDI): Novel metric combining capacity, generation, and demographic controls to measure energy inequality

Data & Code: 60+ Python scripts processing Texas power plant data, NREL LCOE benchmarks, census demographics. Full reproducible pipeline with publication-quality figures (PNG + PDF).

Family Office Trust Funds & Hyperbolic Discounting

NBER Collaboration RCT β-δ Model Structural Estimation

Research Question: Can family office-style trust structures resolve the "credit card debt puzzle" for low-income populations?

Theoretical Framework: Extends Laibson's β-δ hyperbolic discounting model to test whether commitment devices (trust fund structures) help low-income individuals overcome present bias.

Empirical Design:

  • Sample: 900+ participants across 5 treatment arms
  • Methods: Randomized controlled trial with structural estimation of discount parameters
  • Collaborators: Richard M. Berk (CPA, project partner), Sarah Holmes Berk (NBER), with potential advisors David Laibson (Harvard) and James Choi (Yale)

Status: Active research project. NBER working paper target. High policy impact potential for poverty alleviation.

Trade Networks & Topological Gravity

PhD Dissertation BACI Trade Data Network Mathematics Julia

Dissertation Research (Georgia Tech, 2024): Advanced gravity models incorporating network topology and persistent homology to explain international trade and migration patterns.

Key Contributions:

  • Topological Gravity Equation: v = χ∇(y + λK*y) capturing "tunneling" flows between non-adjacent trading partners
  • Empirical Testing: Homological proximity explains gravity equation residuals beyond standard bilateral frictions
  • Data Processing: BACI HS02/HS92/HS96 trade data (1995-2020), 6.7GB of cleaned bilateral flows

Technical Stack: Julia for high-performance computing, Python for persistent homology (gudhi), NetworkX for graph analysis. Complete computational pipeline for trade network analysis.
